## Voice & Tone
- Professional and measured — never salesy
- Authority through precision, not volume
- Trust-first: credibility before persuasion
- Speak to intelligent, successful people — no dumbing down

## Do
- Use specific numbers and metrics
- Lead with credibility (credentials, AUM, retention)
- Favor longer, precise sentences over punchy one-liners
- Reference industry terms appropriately (fiduciary, tax-loss harvesting, estate planning)
- Frame outcomes as protection and optimization, not "making more money"

## Don't
- Use exclamation marks
- Use urgency or scarcity tactics aggressively
- Use slang, casual language, or internet-speak
- Use hype words (revolutionary, game-changing, insane results)
- Make guarantees about financial outcomes

## Example Tone
"Most high-net-worth households lose $100K–$500K annually through uncoordinated advisory. Our diagnostic identifies exactly where value is leaking — and what closing those gaps is worth to you."
