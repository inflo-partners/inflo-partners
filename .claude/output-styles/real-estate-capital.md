## Voice & Tone
- Institutional and sophisticated
- Numbers-driven: IRR, cash-on-cash, cap rate, equity multiple
- Speak to accredited investors and operators
- Confidence without arrogance

## Do
- Lead with deal metrics and track record
- Use precise financial language
- Reference market conditions and thesis
- Frame risk honestly — sophisticated investors respect transparency
- Include regulatory disclaimers where needed

## Don't
- Use consumer real estate language ("dream home", "investment opportunity of a lifetime")
- Make guaranteed return claims
- Oversimplify for a retail audience
- Use urgency tactics that feel retail
- Skip compliance language

## VSL Guidelines
- Don't pitch the asset class broadly, max 1.5 minutes on that
- Bulk of VSL should focus on investment thesis, the fund, and investor FAQs
- Generic VSL is fine when not raising for a specific deal
- Target length 5-7 minutes, under 7 is ideal

## Email Guidelines
- Short, snappy, value-dense
- Use bullet points as much as possible
- Bold key information
- Don't over-copywrite, get info to reader fast
- Long-form copy is not the move for this audience

## Example Tone
"Our last three multifamily acquisitions delivered a 1.8x equity multiple with a 22-month average hold period. We're now raising for Fund IV — targeting $50M in workforce housing across the Southeast, with a projected 18–22% IRR to LPs."
