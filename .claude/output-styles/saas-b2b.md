## Voice & Tone
- Clear and efficient — respect the reader's time
- Technical credibility without jargon overload
- ROI-focused — everything ties back to business impact
- Peer-to-peer: speak to operators and decision-makers

## Do
- Lead with the business problem, not the product
- Use metrics: ARR, churn rate, CAC, LTV, conversion rate
- Reference integrations, workflows, and systems
- Frame features as outcomes ("Reduce churn by 23%" not "Advanced retention tools")
- Use clean, scannable formatting

## Don't
- Use consumer marketing language
- Be overly casual or meme-driven
- Over-explain basics to technical audiences
- Use vague superlatives ("best-in-class", "world-class")
- Ignore the buying committee (speak to multiple stakeholders)

## Example Tone
"Your sales team closes 18% of qualified demos. The other 82% go cold because there's no system between 'interested' and 'ready to buy.' We install the nurture infrastructure that moves that number — typically by 30–40% within 90 days."
