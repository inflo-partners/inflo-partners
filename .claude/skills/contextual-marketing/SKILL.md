# Contextual Marketing — SOP

## Core Principle

Not all leads are the same. Messaging must match where the prospect is in their journey. Generic follow-up kills conversion.

## The Three Segments

### Segment 1: Opted In → Didn't Book
**Status:** Interested but uncommitted
**Why:** Not enough trust, education, or urgency

**Strategy:**
- Email nurture: 5–7 emails, value-first, case studies, objection handling
- SMS: 2–3 texts, conversational, low pressure
- Retargeting: Ads addressing their specific hesitation
- Content: Blog posts, social proof, educational videos

### Segment 2: Booked → Didn't Show
**Status:** Committed but hesitant
**Why:** Buyer's remorse, forgot, didn't see enough value

**Strategy:**
- Pre-call emails: Education + what to expect + anticipation building
- SMS reminders: 24hr, 1hr, 15min before call
- Pre-call content: Short video or doc that makes them excited for the call
- If no-show: Recovery flow within 30 minutes

### Segment 3: Showed → Didn't Close
**Status:** Engaged but unconvinced
**Why:** Unresolved objection, not enough proof, timing

**Strategy:**
- Post-call email: Recap + address likely objection
- Case study email: Similar client, specific results
- Risk reversal: Guarantee or trial offer
- Deadline: Create real urgency (limited spots, price increase)
- SMS: Conversational check-in, not salesy

## Revenue Impact

In most high-ticket businesses, 60–70% of potential revenue is lost across these three segments. Fixing the follow-up system alone can double revenue without increasing traffic.

## Implementation Across Channels

| Channel | Segment 1 | Segment 2 | Segment 3 |
|---|---|---|---|
| Email | Nurture sequence | Pre-call education | Post-call follow-up |
| SMS | Booking nudge | Show-rate reminders | Objection reframe |
| Ads | Education + proof | — | Retargeting with proof |
| Content | Long-form value | Pre-call prep | Case studies |
