# Inflo Partners — Conversion Principles

## The 7-11-4 Rule

Before making a high-ticket purchase decision, a prospect typically needs:
- **7 hours** of exposure to the brand
- **11 meaningful touchpoints** across channels
- **4 hours** of content or messaging consumption

Most businesses skip this entirely and push for commitment too early, resulting in uneducated, skeptical prospects who aren't ready to buy.

## The Conversion System

### Front-End (What the prospect sees first)
- Ads (Meta, Google, YouTube)
- Organic content (YouTube, LinkedIn, Instagram, X)
- Landing pages and funnels
- DMs and outbound messaging
- Video Sales Letters (VSLs)

**Goal**: Capture → Educate → Position → Build trust → Pre-sell

### Back-End (Where revenue is made or lost)
- Lead nurturing sequences
- Trust-building content
- Objection handling before the call
- Show rate optimization systems
- Close rate improvement processes

## Contextual Marketing Segments

Not all leads are the same. Segment and message based on behavior:

| Segment | Status | Strategy |
|---------|--------|----------|
| Opted in, didn't book | Interested but uncommitted | Education + urgency + social proof |
| Booked, didn't show | Committed but hesitant | Pre-call value + reminder sequences + objection handling |
| Showed, didn't close | Engaged but unconvinced | Post-call follow-up + case studies + risk reversal |

Each segment receives different messaging, follow-ups, and content to recover lost revenue.

## Key Metrics (Revenue-Focused)

- **Booked call rate**: % of leads who schedule a call
- **Show rate**: % of booked leads who actually show up
- **Close rate**: % of shown leads who buy
- **CAC**: Cost per acquisition
- **Cash collected**: Actual revenue received
- **Revenue per lead**: Total revenue / total leads
- **LTV**: Lifetime value of a client

Everything ties to **revenue outcomes**, not vanity metrics (impressions, likes, followers).

## Copy Framework for High-Ticket

1. **Lead with the problem** — Make them feel understood
2. **Agitate the pain** — Show the cost of inaction
3. **Introduce the system** — Not features, the mechanism
4. **Show proof** — Specific numbers, real results
5. **Handle objections** — Preemptively address hesitations
6. **CTA** — Clear, specific, low-friction next step
