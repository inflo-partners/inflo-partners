# Email Sequence Framework — SOP

## Sequence Types & Timing

| Type | Emails | Spacing | Trigger |
|---|---|---|---|
| Pre-call nurture | 3–7 | Every 1–2 days | Opted in, didn't book |
| Pre-call education | 2–3 | Day before, morning of | Booked a call |
| Post-call follow-up | 3–5 | Days 1, 2, 4, 7 | Showed, didn't close |
| Re-engagement | 2–3 | Days 14, 21, 30 | Gone cold |

## Email Structure

1. **Subject line**: Under 50 chars, curiosity or benefit-driven
2. **Opening hook**: 1–2 sentences, pattern interrupt or relatable pain
3. **Body**: One idea per email, value-first
4. **CTA**: Single clear action, low friction

## Subject Line Formulas
- "[First name], quick question about [topic]"
- "The #1 reason [audience] struggle with [problem]"
- "[Specific result] in [timeframe] — here's how"
- "Are you making this [topic] mistake?"

## Copy Rules
- Short paragraphs (1–3 sentences max)
- Write like a person, not a brand
- One idea per email
- Always deliver value before asking for action
- Proof and specifics over vague claims

## Sequence Progression Logic
- Email 1: Acknowledge + framework introduction
- Email 2: Proof / case study
- Email 3: Objection handling
- Email 4: Additional value
- Email 5: Direct CTA with urgency

## Real Estate / Capital Raises (real-estate-capital)
When the client's output style is `real-estate-capital`, override the default copy rules:
- Short, snappy, value-dense, no fluff
- Use bullet points as much as possible
- Bold key information (metrics, dates, returns, fund details)
- Don't over-copywrite, get info to reader fast
- Long-form copy is not the move for this audience
- Investors skim, structure every email for scannability
- The standard "story-based" email style does not apply to this vertical
