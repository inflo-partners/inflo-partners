## Call Analysis — Sales Patterns

### Call Structure (25 min)
1. **Rapport & location** (0:00–0:30)
2. **Frame the call** — "break down what's going on, walk through bottlenecks, talk next steps" (0:30–1:00)
3. **Discovery questions** — let them talk about the business (1:00–3:40)
4. **Establish credibility** — "I studied finance, worked for a GP" (3:40–4:00)
5. **Diagnose the problem** — researched their ads beforehand, identified lack of front-end conversion (4:00–6:00)
6. **Probe current process** — sales process, show rates, follow-up (6:00–9:15)
7. **Identify data gaps** — no metrics on show rate, no tracking (9:15–10:00)
8. **Show the solution** — screen share of sample landing page + post-call booking page (10:00–14:45)
9. **Probe current vendor pain** — ad manager issues (14:45–18:45)
10. **Present pricing** — $5k setup + $2k/mo ad management (18:45–20:00)
11. **Handle "who will I work with" objection** — "that would be 100% me" (20:00–20:45)
12. **Recap deliverables** — detailed list of everything included (20:45–22:00)
13. **Soft close** — prospect self-closes: "I think we're definitely going to move forward" (22:00–22:30)
14. **Add value / future-pace** — SDRs, long-term vision, "asset you keep forever" (22:30–23:30)
15. **Book follow-up** — Friday at 11am with Guy included (23:30–25:15)

### Key Sales Moves
- **Did research BEFORE the call** — pulled up their Facebook ads library, reviewed their current funnel
- **Diagnosed the problem immediately** — "lack of a front end conversion mechanism"
- **Used his own system as proof** — showed his own landing page and post-call booking page as examples
- **Let the prospect sell themselves** — didn't push hard, let Nicki identify her own pain points
- **Positioned as educator, not salesperson** — "I'm just here to provide value"
- **"Asset you keep forever"** — reframes the cost as an investment, not an expense
- **Founder-led delivery** — "that would be 100% me" eliminated the trust objection instantly
- **Didn't push ad management** — focused on the infrastructure first, left ads as optional
- **Offered to write out deliverables** — gave Nicki a tool to sell Guy internally

### Objections Surfaced
1. **"Who will I be working with?"** → "100% me, I run a tight ship, 5-10 clients max"
2. **Current vendor disappointment** — bad creative, AI-looking, not diverse → Shariq didn't bash, just offered to take over
3. **Need to talk to partner (Guy)** → Booked follow-up with both, offered written deliverables to help Nicki explain

### Language That Worked
- "There's a lack of a front end conversion mechanism"
- "It's usually an education issue"
- "That's an asset that you guys will keep for your company forever"
- "We're completely platform agnostic"
- "I run a pretty tight ship"
- "I'm very founder focused"
- "I'm just here to provide value to you guys"
