## [~2:30] THE RULE — 7-11-4 Framework

Before I explain the conversion system, I wanna explain one core marketing rule that explains what we do and why we do it. It's called the 7-11-4 rule.

This comes from research conducted by Google on the buying behavior of digital consumers.

What this states is if there is a digital consumer, they must:
- See your brand 7 times
- Engage with you across 11 touch points
- Interact with 4 hours of either messaging or videos

To be able to make a buying decision and actually be ready to purchase your products.

So let's think about that. Let's say someone clicks on your ad, lands on your page and you immediately ask them to book a call to discuss a $5,000, $10,000 or even $100,000+ dollar engagement. What are the chances they not only show up to the call but are actually educated and ready enough to pay you?

Basically, zero.

Despite this, many companies we see are still doing thousands a month without a conversion system. But the whole problem is they're leaving thousands of dollars more on the table.

## [~5:00] THE FRONT END

The front end is everything your prospect sees especially when they first interact with your business.

- Others (especially B2B enterprise) use funnels to get people through the door to book meetings, investment meetings, demos, or discovery calls

## [~8:30] THE BACK END SYSTEM — What We Build

We build a back end selling infrastructure — the infrastructure needed to nurture, educate, and market prospects until they're ready to book a call or show up pre-sold and ready to pay.

### Step 1: Capture the Correct Data

As soon as someone is interested, we build opt-in systems to collect not just emails but phone numbers.

Why phone numbers matter:
- Email open rates: 20%
- SMS open rates: 70%
- Phone numbers allow us to get right in front of the lead

### Step 2: Immediately Trigger Automation Sequences

The moment someone gives us their information:

- **SDR outreach** — team of SDRs get notified and reach out automatically to pre-qualify, pre-vet, and educate the lead before the call
- **Value dense email nurture** — educate them on the offer, who you are, what you offer, why you're better than competitors, answer all objections before the call
- **SMS flows** — get right in front of their phone, send Google Meet or Zoom link directly, keep show rate at 80-90% minimum
- **Retargeting pixel fires** — stay top of mind if running paid traffic

### Step 3: The Nurture Process Commences

Over the next 24-72 hours, automated email flows educate leads based on where they are contextually in the sales process.

**This is contextual marketing:**

Different buckets get different marketing:
- Opted in but not booked → specific sequence to get them to book
- Booked, pre-call → specific pre-call education sequence
- Booked but no-showed → specific no-show recovery sequence
- Showed but didn't close → specific follow-up sequence

We also:
- Build long form sales assets sent pre-call to handle objections (10-20 min YouTube videos on who you are, your offer, past success)
- SDRs call and text leads to pre-qualify them
- SMS messages maintain conversational momentum (not generic "your call is in an hour" — actual conversations to keep leads warm)

## VSL Structure Notes (Voice Reference)

**Total length:** ~16-17 minutes (long VSL for cold traffic)

**Structure used:**
1. Hook — identify audience + bottleneck (0:00)
2. Intro — who is Shariq / Inflo Partners (0:45)
3. Proof — results with real numbers (1:15)
4. Framework — 7-11-4 rule education (2:30)
5. Problem — what's missing and why (3:30)
6. Solution overview — front end + back end (4:30)
7. Front end explanation (5:00)
8. Back end explanation + pain agitation (6:00)
9. The system — 3 steps (8:30)
10. Outcome — what changes (12:00)
11. Math — before vs after (12:30)
12. What we install — deliverables list (14:00)
13. CTA — book discovery call (15:30)

**Voice patterns in VSL:**
- Conversational, not scripted-sounding (uses "right?" as verbal confirmation throughout)
- Teaches the framework openly — gives away the system so prospects see the gap
- Uses math to make the case undeniable ($10k vs $100k same spend)
- Positions Inflo as a partner, not an agency
- Frames the call as an audit, not a pitch
- Handles the "why should I trust you" objection early with proof
- Self-aware: "I don't say this to brag"
- Low-pressure CTA: "If not, I'll still point you in the right direction"
