## Email 2 — Education + System Breakdown

**Trigger:** 6 hours after call is booked

---

Hey {{invitee_first_name}},

Most high-ticket businesses operate like this:

1. Generate attention (ads, content, referrals)
2. Send traffic to a Calendly or generic landing page
3. Hope people book a call
4. Close on the phone

Seems logical, right?

Well here's the problem:

Between step 2 and step 3, you're leaking 70% of your revenue.

Why?

Because you're missing a conversion system — the infrastructure that captures attention, qualifies intent, and forces education in the process.

Let me explain...

Most businesses send traffic directly to a calendar link or generic application page without any data collection or qualification system.

What happens because of this?

* Leads drop off because booking a call is high commitment
* Calendar links sit empty because people "aren't ready yet"
* Follow-up is inconsistent or nonexistent
* Sales reps waste time on unqualified calls
* You have no idea which leads are serious and which are browsing

You're treating a $5,000–$100,000+ decision like an e-commerce checkout.

It just simply doesn't work.

Here's what a proper high-ticket conversion system looks like:

Step 1: Capture Name, Email, AND Phone Number

Instead of sending leads directly to a calendar, make sure you collect phone numbers.

You need phone numbers because:

* It allows manual SDR outreach (text, call, voicemail)
* It enables SMS follow-up (70%+ open rates vs. 20% for email)
* It signals higher intent (people don't give their number unless they're serious)

Step 2: Trigger Immediate Multi-Channel Nurture

The moment someone opts in:

* Email #1 fires instantly (confirmation + next step)
* SMS #1 fires within 5 minutes (personal, conversational)
* SDR gets notified (manual touch point within 5 minutes)
* Retargeting pixel fires (keeps you top-of-mind)

Step 3: Manual Outreach + Automated Touchpoints

Over the next 24–72 hours:

* Automated email sequences educate and build intent (like this sequence you're in right now)
* SMS messages to maintain conversational momentum
* SDR calls/texts pre-qualifies leads and book calls on the spot
* Retargeting ads keep your brand present across every platform they visit

Now by the time the prospect books a call and shows up, they:

* Understand what you do and who you are (educated)
* Know why they need it (intent-shaped)
* Are pre-qualified (SDR vetted)
* Show up ready to buy (not "just exploring")

This is the difference between a 15% close rate and a 40% close rate.

Same traffic. Same offer. Different infrastructure.

On our call, we'll map out a conversion system specific to your offering.

We'll show you exactly:

* How to structure your opt-in to capture phone numbers without killing conversions
* What your nurture sequence should look like (email + SMS)
* How SDR outreach fits into the flow (manual + automated)
* What happens in the 24–72 hours before a sales call (the most critical window)

Whether you're getting referrals, running paid traffic, or have an organic following — if you're not utilizing a proper conversion mechanism chances are you're leaving more revenue on the table than you realized.

But don't worry, that's exactly what our call is for. We'll discuss how we can implement steps 1-3 above to fix that for good.

Talk soon,

Shariq

## Sequence Notes

- 3 emails total across ~24 hours
- Email 1: Confirm + frame the call as high-value + pre-handle objections via FAQ
- Email 2: Educate on the system (teach while you sell) + demonstrate expertise
- Email 3: Set expectations + request they come prepared with numbers + logistics
- Meta move: The sequence itself IS the product in action — they experience the nurture system while being sold on it
- Each email has a distinct job — no overlap
