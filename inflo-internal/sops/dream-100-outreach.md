# Dream 100 Outreach SOP

This is the complete standard operating procedure for Dream 100 outbound prospecting at Inflo Partners. If you're reading this, your job is to find high-ticket businesses, build them a free deliverable, send it to them, and follow up until they reply. Once they reply, you hand it off to Shariq.

Read this entire document before you start any work.

---

## 1. What Inflo Partners Does

Inflo Partners is a revenue infrastructure partner. We are not a marketing agency.

We work with established businesses doing $10K to $100K+ per month that already have traffic, leads, or demand but don't have the conversion system to turn it into predictable revenue.

Most of these businesses are running ads, building an audience, or getting referrals. They don't need more traffic. They need a system that converts it.

We install everything between "someone saw your ad" and "they paid you." That includes landing pages, email and SMS sequences, CRM automation, sales process infrastructure, retargeting, and more.

The core principle behind everything we do is the **7-11-4 Rule**: before someone makes a high-ticket buying decision, they typically need 7 hours of exposure to your brand, 11 meaningful touchpoints, and 4 hours of content consumption. Most businesses skip all of that and wonder why their close rate is low.

**What is the Dream 100 strategy?**

We identify businesses that are a perfect fit for what we do. Instead of pitching them, we send value upfront. We create a full breakdown of their current setup (what's working, what's broken, what's costing them the most revenue) along with a free landing page they can actually use. We deliver this through a Gamma document.

We track when they open the doc. Then we follow up.

**Your role**: Find the leads, create the deliverable, send the initial outreach, follow up based on whether they opened the doc, and hand it off the moment they reply. You don't close deals. You don't get on calls. You get the conversation started.

## 4. Phase 1: Finding Leads

This is where every outreach cycle starts. The flow goes: pick a niche, generate keywords, search Ads Library, use Gemini to pull leads, qualify, check CRM, document.

### Step 1: Pick a Niche from the 500 Niches Sheet

Go to the 500 Niches Sheet: https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1RTGFdhXlpNswSv0y9piadLu_O3cnbKGSz1UMj-3UzIc/edit?usp=sharing

This sheet has 500 niches, not keywords. Each niche is a category like "wealth management" or "business coaching."

Shariq will help you choose which niches to target in the beginning. Don't just pick random ones.

### Step 2: Generate Keywords Using ChatGPT

Open a fresh ChatGPT chat and **PIN it**. You will reuse this chat often.

Take the niche you picked from the sheet and paste it into the prompt below. ChatGPT will give you 20-40 keywords grouped by category that you can copy-paste directly into Facebook Ads Library.

**ChatGPT Keyword Generation Prompt:**

```
You are helping me generate high-quality search keywords to use inside the Facebook Ads Library to find potential clients for my marketing agency.

I will give you a niche. Your job is to generate a list of keywords that will help me find companies in that niche that are actively running ads and selling high-ticket services, offers, or investments.

Niche:
[INSERT NICHE HERE]

---

Your goal is to generate keywords that will surface companies that:
- Are running ads
- Are selling something (not hiring or just posting content)
- Likely have high customer value (can afford marketing services)
- Are trying to generate leads (calls, applications, webinars, etc.)

---

Instructions:

1. Generate 20–40 keyword variations based on the niche.

2. Include different intent angles:
- Core service keywords (e.g., "[niche] company", "[niche] firm")
- Buyer intent keywords (e.g., "[niche] consultation", "[niche] services")
- Lead generation keywords (e.g., "[niche] webinar", "[niche] book a call", "[niche] apply")
- Investment / high-ticket keywords (if applicable)
- Problem/solution keywords related to the niche
- Alternative ways people describe the same niche

3. Focus on keywords that are likely to show companies making money, NOT low-budget or consumer searches.

4. Avoid keywords that would surface:
- Job listings
- Educational content only (unless tied to selling)
- Low-ticket products
- Irrelevant audiences

---

5. Output format:

Group the keywords into categories:

Core Keywords:
-

Buyer Intent Keywords:
-

Lead Generation Keywords:
-

High-Ticket / Investment Keywords:
-

Alternative / Related Keywords:
-

---

6. Keep everything simple and directly usable.
These keywords should be ready to copy and paste into Facebook Ads Library.

Do NOT explain anything. Only output the keyword lists.
```

### Step 3: Search Facebook Ads Library

Go to https://www.facebook.com/ads/library

Set the filters:
- Country: **United States**
- Ad category: **All Ads**

Search one keyword at a time from the ChatGPT output and work through the results.

### Step 4: Use Gemini in Google Chrome to Pull Leads

**Important: Use Google Chrome for all prospecting.** Gemini is built into Chrome and you can access it from the top-left corner. It will save you a massive amount of time pulling lead info from the Ads Library results.

After searching a keyword in Ads Library, hit the Gemini icon in Chrome and use this prompt:

**Gemini Lead Extraction Prompt:**

```
I just searched Facebook Ads Library for [INSERT NICHE HERE]. Please review the ads and generate a list of leads that fit the following criteria:

Lead Criteria:
* High-ticket service-based businesses
* Actively looking for high-ticket clients or investors (depending on niche)
* Employee count between 1 and 50
* Disqualify large institutions or firms with hundreds or thousands of employees
* Must have a live landing page or website linked in the ad

For each valid lead, provide:
1. Company Name
2. Website / Landing Page URL
3. Email Address (if listed in the ad or on the landing page)

Only include leads that meet the criteria above. The company name and website are required, email is optional.

Extra context:
I partner with high-ticket service-based businesses and build full-stack acquisition and conversion systems for them. Focus on leads where our services would be relevant — businesses with existing demand but no in-house team large enough to fully handle lead acquisition and conversion.
```

### Step 5: Evaluate the Lead

You're looking for businesses that match our ideal client profile. Here's what qualifies a lead:

**Green lights (good lead):**
- Selling a high-ticket offer ($2,000+)
- The ad drives to a landing page or funnel (not just a social media post)
- They're spending real money on ads (multiple ad variations running)
- US-based business
- Has a personal brand or company brand behind it
- Falls in one of our target verticals:
  - Coaching and consulting
  - Financial advisory and wealth management
  - Real estate, syndication, and capital allocation
  - SaaS and B2B services
  - Online education and info products

**Red flags (skip these):**
- E-commerce or physical product businesses
- Low-ticket offers (under $500)
- Ads running for less than 7 days (probably just testing)
- International businesses outside the US
- MLM or network marketing
- No website or landing page at all

### Step 6: Check Close CRM Before Anything Else

Before you do anything with a lead, search them in Close CRM.

- Search by the person's name AND the business name
- If they're already in the CRM and we've sent them a deliverable, **skip them**
- **Exception:** If they previously responded to our outreach but the conversation went cold, flag them for potential re-engagement. Add a note in the CRM but do NOT re-send the same deliverable without checking with Shariq first

This step is non-negotiable. Duplicate outreach makes us look unprofessional.

### Step 7: Add the Lead to Close and Create the Deliverable

Once you have a qualified lead:

1. **Add their email to Close CRM.** This is covered in detail in Loom 2 (Finding Leads and Saving in Close CRM). Follow that process exactly.
2. **Go to Phase 2 and create the deliverable** using the Tally form deliverable generator.

That's it. Don't overthink this step. Get the lead into Close, then move straight to building the deliverable.

## 6. Phase 3: Initial Outreach

This is where you send the first message. Every detail here matters.

### Rules Before You Send Anything

1. **Every email is sent from Close CRM.** All outbound goes directly from Close, never from your personal email. This is covered in the Loom on finding leads and saving in Close CRM.
2. **Personalization is mandatory.** Reference something specific about their ad, their landing page, or their offer. Never send a generic template without editing it.
3. **Never pitch on the first message.** We're leading with value. No mention of pricing, packages, or "working together."

### Step 1: Send the Initial Email from Close

**Subject line** (pick whichever fits best):
- `quick breakdown of your [their offer type] funnel`
- `put together something for [Business Name]`
- `[First Name], noticed something about your ads`

**Email body:**

```
Hey [First Name],

I came across your [specific ad reference, e.g. "Meta ad for your wealth management practice"] and wanted to reach out.

I took a look at your current setup and noticed [your specific observation, e.g. "you're driving traffic straight to a booking page without a pre-qualification step" or "your landing page is doing a lot of heavy lifting but there's no follow-up system once someone opts in"].

I went ahead and put together a full breakdown of your current funnel, what's most likely costing you the most in lost revenue, and some free assets you can plug in right away.

Here's the full breakdown: [Gamma Link]

There's also a free landing page in there that I built specifically for your offer. You can use it as-is or tweak it however you want.

No strings attached, just thought it'd be useful.

Let me know what you think.

Shariq
```

**What to personalize every time:**
- The ad reference (Meta ad, Google ad, YouTube ad, etc.)
- The specific observation (pulled from your Phase 1 research)
- The offer type (wealth management, coaching, consulting, etc.)

### Step 2: Log Everything in Close CRM

After sending the email:

1. Make sure the prospect is in Close CRM
2. Go into the lead's record and **update the opportunity to "Deliverable Sent"** (if you didn't already do this in Phase 2, Step 6)
3. Log the email as an activity (Close does this automatically when you send from Close)
4. Set a follow-up task for 24 hours later
5. Add a note with the Gamma link and your observation about their funnel

## 7. Phase 4: Follow-Up Sequences

This is where most outreach campaigns fail. Most people send one email and give up. We don't do that.

Your follow-up depends on one thing: **did they open the Gamma doc or not?**

Gamma sends a notification when the doc is opened. That notification is your trigger.

Use the Close Smart Views to manage your follow-up workflow:
- **FU 1 NEEDED** — check this view daily for leads needing first follow-up
- **FU 2 NEEDED** — check this view daily for leads needing second follow-up
- **Deliverable Sent/Consumed** — track where each lead is in the process

### When the Doc is Opened: Update CRM Immediately

The moment you see a Gamma open notification:

1. Go to Close CRM
2. Find the lead
3. Move them from **"Deliverable Sent"** to **"Deliverable Consumed"** in the Sales Pipeline
4. Then immediately send Follow-Up #1 below

---

### Branch A: They Opened the Doc

**Speed-to-lead is everything here.** The moment you get the open notification, send the follow-up. Not in an hour. Not after lunch. Immediately.

**Follow-Up #1 (send immediately when you get the open notification)**

Reply in the same email thread from Close. Do not start a new thread.

```
Hey [First Name], just saw you checked out the breakdown.

Curious to hear your initial thoughts on it.

Anything in there that stood out?

Shariq
```

If they reply: **Stop. Hand off to Shariq immediately.** (See Phase 5)

If no reply within 24 hours, send Follow-Up #2.

---

**Follow-Up #2 (24 hours after Follow-Up #1, no reply)**

Reply in the same thread.

```
Hey [First Name],

Wanted to follow up on the breakdown I sent over.

The main thing I flagged was [restate your specific observation from the initial email, e.g. "the gap between your ad traffic and your booking system"]. That's usually where the biggest revenue leak is for businesses running your type of offer.

Happy to walk you through it on a quick call if that'd be helpful. No pitch, just want to make sure you're getting the most out of what I put together.

Shariq
```

If they reply: **Stop. Hand off to Shariq.**

If no reply within 3-4 days, send Follow-Up #3.

---

**Follow-Up #3 (3-4 days after Follow-Up #2, still no reply)**

Reply in the same thread.

```
Hey [First Name],

Last follow up on this.

I built that landing page and breakdown specifically for [Business Name] so I want to make sure it doesn't just sit in your inbox.

If the timing isn't right, totally get it. But if you're open to a 15 minute call to walk through it, I think you'd find it valuable.

Either way, the assets are yours to keep.

Shariq
```

---

### Branch B: They Have NOT Opened the Doc

If 24-48 hours pass and no Gamma open notification has come through, the goal shifts. Now you need to get them to actually open it.

**Follow-Up #1 (24-48 hours after initial email, doc not opened)**

Reply in the same email thread from Close.

```

Just wanted to make sure this didn't get buried.

I put together a full breakdown of your [their offer type] funnel, including where you're most likely leaving revenue on the table and a free landing page I built for your offer.

Here's the link again in case you missed it: [Gamma Link]

Worth a look, takes about 5 minutes.

Shariq
```

---

**Follow-Up #2 (3-4 days after Follow-Up #1, doc still not opened)**

```
Hey [First Name],

One more nudge on this.

I spent some time going through your [ad/funnel/offer] and put together a pretty detailed breakdown of what's working, what's not, and what I'd change.

There's also a free landing page in there built specifically for [Business Name].

[Gamma Link]

Totally free, no catch. Just wanted to get it in front of you.

Shariq
```

---

**Follow-Up #3 (7 days after initial email, final message)**

```
Hey [First Name],

Last message on this.

I built a full funnel breakdown and free landing page for [Business Name] that I haven't heard back on. Figured I'd send it one more time in case the timing was off.

[Gamma Link]

If it's not a good time, no worries at all. The assets are yours whenever you're ready.
```

## 8. Phase 5: Handoff

Your job ends the moment a prospect replies. Here's exactly what happens.

### When a prospect replies (to any email):

1. **Do NOT reply.** Do not write anything back to them, even if they ask a simple question.
2. **Go to Slack immediately** and send Shariq a message with:
   - Prospect's name and business name
   - Which email they replied to (initial outreach, follow-up #1, etc.)
   - Copy/paste their exact reply
   - Link to their lead record in Close CRM
3. **Update Close CRM** with the reply logged as an activity

Shariq takes over from there. He'll respond in the same email thread and work to get them on a call.

### What you never do:

- Never respond to the prospect yourself
- Never get on a phone call with a prospect
- Never promise anything beyond the free deliverable
- Never discuss pricing, packages, timelines, or "working together"
- Never send follow-ups after the prospect has replied (Shariq handles it from here)

## 9. Key Principles

Print this out. Reference it daily.

1. **Speed-to-lead is everything.** When a Gamma notification comes in, follow up immediately. Not in 30 minutes. Now.

2. **This is a touchpoint game.** Email + Gamma doc + free landing page = multiple touchpoints. Every interaction builds familiarity and trust.

3. **The deliverable does the selling.** Your job is to get it in front of them. The Gamma doc and free landing page do the persuading.

4. **Never pitch on the first message.** Every outreach leads with value. No mention of pricing, packages, or working together until after they reply and Shariq takes over.

5. **Always personalize.** Reference their specific ad, offer, or landing page. If you can't find something specific to say about their business, you haven't done enough research.

6. **Check CRM before every outreach.** Search Close by name AND business name. Duplicates waste time and make us look sloppy.

7. **Follow up until they reply.** Silence is not a "no." Most conversations start after the 3rd to 5th touchpoint.

8. **Hand off the moment they reply.** Slack Shariq immediately. Do not try to keep the conversation going yourself.

9. **Always update CRM pipeline stages.** Every lead starts at "Deliverable Sent." The moment they open the doc, move them to "Deliverable Consumed." Keep the pipeline accurate at all times.
