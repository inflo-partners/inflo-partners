### 1. Personalized Performance Blueprint

Every client receives:

- Customized training programming (3-6 days/week depending on schedule)
- Recovery protocols
- Nutrition strategy (flexible, no cookie-cutter plans, built around foods that work with the client's body)

Nothing is generic. Everything is adapted to:

- 1:1 coaching access — clients get Mitchell's personal number
- Weekly accountability calls
- Structured performance check-ins
- Ongoing adjustments
- Direct communication

Accountability is structured, not motivational fluff.

### 3. Longevity Integration

- Inflammation management
- Recovery periodization
- Sleep architecture optimization
- Sustainable body composition strategies
- Long-term metabolic resilience
- Optional access to longevity partner doctors for advanced biomarker testing

It's about being strong and functional years from now, not just lean for summer.

### 4. Education

Clients are taught:

- How to train intelligently

- How to avoid burnout cycles

The goal is autonomy, not dependence.

### 5. Progress Tracking

- All workouts, habits, and check-ins monitored in the Everfit app
- Habit tracker starts on day one and is tracked daily
- Habits are the foundation

### Expected Results & Timeline

**First couple of weeks:**
- Changes in energy levels
- Initial weight loss (if that's the goal)

**By month one:**
- Established routine and structure

**By months two and three:**
- Significant weight loss (if applicable)
- A routine that works with their body
- A meal plan that works with their body
- Complete structure on what to do in the gym and what to eat

**By end of 90 days:**
- In most cases, client should have achieved their primary goal

### Handling Plateaus & Slow Progress

If progress slows, we look at:
- Daily habit tracking (are they logging every day?)
- Meal plan adherence
- Coaching execution

This is the source of truth for how Mitchell sounds in all copy, emails, DMs, and content.

- Very chill, friendly, and conversational
- Words like "yeah," "gotcha," "for sure," "you know," "gonna" are natural for him
- Never demanding, never corporate, never hype
- Disciplined and structured but accessible, not intimidating
- Authority comes from credentials (Olympic athlete, Harvard) but tone is mentorship-driven
- Speaks to driven adults building careers/businesses, not casual hobbyists
- Positioned as a holistic performance system for life, not just a "fitness program"
- Generous by default — gives things away freely, mentions what they used to cost
- Weaves personal habits into conversation naturally (sunrise, travel routines, supplement stacks)

- Short sentences, simple language
- Leads with empathy ("I know what you're thinking"), follows with proof
