Frequently Asked Questions

The Momentum Wealth Fund is a private real estate investment vehicle focused on acquiring, operating, and improving self-storage facilities across the United States. The fund provides accredited investors with exposure to a diversified portfolio of self-storage assets managed by experienced operators.
Participation in the Momentum Wealth Fund is limited to accredited investors as defined by SEC regulations. All investors must complete third-party verification of their accredited status before participation. This requirement ensures compliance with Rule 506(c) of Regulation D.
The minimum investment amount is $100,000. This threshold may be adjusted at the discretion of the fund managers. Please schedule an investment briefing to discuss current investment parameters and eligibility requirements.
The fund seeks competitive risk-adjusted returns based on historical performance data and prevailing market conditions within the self-storage sector. Return targets are informed by industry benchmarks and operational projections but are not guaranteed. All investments carry risk, including the potential loss of principal.
Self-storage has historically demonstrated resilience across various economic cycles due to several demand drivers including life transitions, business storage needs, and urbanization trends. The asset class offers operational flexibility with month-to-month leases, relatively low tenant turnover costs, and scalable management systems. However, past performance is not indicative of future results, and market conditions can change.
The fund charges an annual management fee based on assets under management and operates with a preferred return structure for investors. Detailed fee information, including the management fee percentage, preferred return rate, and profit split structure, is provided in the Private Placement Memorandum. All fees are structured to align manager and investor interests.
The management team has transacted on 29 self-storage facilities across 17 states, representing significant experience in acquisition, repositioning, and operations within the self-storage sector. The team brings backgrounds in real estate investment, property management, and financial analysis.
Real estate investments may offer certain tax considerations such as depreciation deductions and the potential for cost segregation studies to accelerate depreciation. These strategies can impact the timing and character of taxable income. This information is educational only and not tax advice. Investors should consult qualified tax professionals regarding their specific situations.

The fund allows accredited investors to gain exposure to a diversified self-storage portfolio rather than owning and operating a single facility themselves.
